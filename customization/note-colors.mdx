Note colors are one of the most powerful ways to organize your Stickee board visually. Instead of reading every note to understand its context, a consistent color system lets you scan your board in seconds and know exactly what needs your attention. Stickee gives you 10 distinct colors to work with.

## Available Colors

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="🟡 Yellow" icon="note-sticky">
    The classic sticky note look. Use it for general, uncategorized notes — your catch-all default.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🩷 Pink" icon="lightbulb">
    Personal items, ideas, and creative thoughts. Great for brainstorming sessions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🔵 Blue" icon="briefcase">
    Work tasks and professional notes. Helps keep career-related items distinct from personal ones.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🟢 Green" icon="circle-check">
    Completed items or anything with a positive outcome. A satisfying color to reach.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🟣 Purple" icon="star">
    High-priority items or special projects that deserve extra attention.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🟠 Orange" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Urgent items or warnings. Use sparingly so the urgency signal stays meaningful.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🩵 Teal" icon="link">
    Reference material and links. Everything you need to look up or come back to.
  </Card>
  <Card title="💜 Lavender" icon="leaf">
    Soft and calming. Ideal for non-urgent, low-pressure items you'll get to eventually.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🍑 Peach" icon="users">
    Meetings, follow-ups, and people-related notes. Perfect for anything involving collaboration.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🌿 Mint" icon="seedling">
    Fresh ideas and brainstorming notes. Use it when something is new and still taking shape.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Setting a Note Color

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="New Notes">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Add Note dialog">
        Click the **+ New Note** button or press <kbd>N</kbd> to open the note creation dialog.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Choose a color">
        Click the color picker in the dialog and select any of the 10 available colors. The preview updates immediately so you can see exactly how your note will look.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create the note">
        Fill in your content and click **Save**. Your note appears on the board in the chosen color.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Existing Notes">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the note">
        Click on any note on your board to open its detail view.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open the color picker">
        Inside the note detail, click the **color picker** control. The 10 color swatches appear for selection.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select a new color">
        Click any color swatch to apply it. The change saves automatically and updates your board immediately.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Color Strategy Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Assign one color per project or context">
    Pick a dedicated color for each major project or area of your life — blue for work, pink for personal, teal for research. Once you establish this system, switching contexts on your board becomes effortless.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Reserve orange and purple for urgent or high-priority items">
    These two colors naturally draw the eye. Keep them meaningful by using them only when something is genuinely urgent (orange) or strategically important (purple). If every note is orange, none of them are.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keep yellow as your default catch-all">
    Use yellow for quick notes, miscellaneous thoughts, and anything you haven't categorized yet. It acts as an inbox — you can always re-color a yellow note once you know where it belongs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Filter by color using the search bar">
    Stickee's search understands color names. Type **blue** in the search bar to instantly filter your board to all blue notes, **orange** to see only urgent items, and so on. This makes color-based organization incredibly fast to navigate.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>
  You can search for notes by color name in the search bar — for example, searching **"teal"** shows only your teal reference notes, and **"green"** surfaces all your completed items. Combine this with content search terms for even more precise filtering.
</Info>

<Tip>
  Consistent color usage is the single biggest upgrade you can make to your board's readability. Once your system is set, you can understand the state of your entire workspace at a glance — without reading a single word.
</Tip>
